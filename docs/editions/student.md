# Student edition

The student edition is the reader-facing PDF. It is not a workbook disguised as a tax form. It is a short language magazine with lessons, reactions and appendices.

Each lesson begins with a compact text: sometimes a monologue, sometimes a dialogue, sometimes a vocabulary study or an exercise. The topic may be cultural, but the real subject is usually language under pressure.

## voices after the lesson

The voices are not mascots. They are recurring ways of noticing language.

Ines listens for phrasework that sounds alive. Camila reads the past like an archive. Piotr looks for argumentative bridges. Rafa asks whether anybody would say the sentence after missing a bus. Other personas appear when the lesson needs their particular kind of trouble.

## moods

Moods are shorter. They are not characters with biographies. They are quick emotional readings: amused, analytical, bored, confused, impressed, irritated, sad and twisted.

A mood may reduce a grand idea to dinner and rain. Another may notice the sad part before anyone else wants to admit it. Twisted usually finds the basement door.

## class chronicle

At the end of each lesson package, the class chronicle records what the broader cast made of the lesson.

It is part summary, part commentary, part small institutional rumour. The chronicle helps the edition feel continuous: the same voices keep returning, carrying their obsessions with them like suspicious luggage.

## why appendices matter

Topicalia does not try to become a complete grammar reference. Instead, it indexes what appears inside the edition.

If a connector, idiom or grammar pattern is marked in context, it can later appear in an appendix. The appendix is therefore not a shopping list of everything learners should know. It is a map of what this edition actually used.
