# moods

Moods are not stable voices.

They appear briefly, register a reaction, and disappear without accumulating history or personality. Where personas return and develop habits, moods interrupt, distort, or intensify a single moment.

They do not explain the lesson. They lean on it, sometimes lightly, sometimes with enough pressure to make it shift.

Because of that, moods remain simple in form, but their effect changes with level: at A1 they stay close to basic statements, while at higher levels they begin to stretch tone, implication, or structure without fully committing to either.
